What happens to you when you stop smoking?

Quitting smoking is not something you can do in a quarter of an hour. It is a long-term process that entails different reactions over a certain period of time. Some of them are of a psychological or emotional nature, while others are direct withdrawal symptoms, because the body suddenly has to do without a certain strong substance - nicotine. Those who turn it on even before they swing their legs out of bed in the morning are the ones who have the biggest withdrawal problems. The first nicotine doses of the day stimulate the brain and central nervous system, while later doses can have a calming effect. This explains why those trying to quit smoking often experience two different kinds of sleep disturbances. Drowsiness during the day is a common phenomenon - while at the same time it can be difficult to sleep at night.
